SCIENTIFIC CONCLUSION
================================================================================

HYPOTHESIS: E:I ratio correlates with glucose metabolism

RESULT: **HYPOTHESIS NOT SUPPORTED**

With correct anatomical classification, there is NO relationship between
E:I ratio and rCMRGlc in human cortex.

KEY OBSERVATIONS:
  • Occipital lobe has HIGHEST E:I (3.18) but NOT highest metabolism
  • Parietal and Occipital have SAME metabolism (35.8) but different E:I
  • No correlation at lobe level (r=0.127, p=0.838)
  • No correlation at subregion level (r=0.073, p=0.761)

LIMITATION:
  We assign same lobe-level rCMRGlc to all subregions within that lobe.
  If V1C truly has higher metabolism than V2/A19 (as Heiss suggested with
  the 40.1 value), we cannot detect this with lobe-averaged data.

  To properly test this hypothesis, need SUBREGION-SPECIFIC metabolic data.
